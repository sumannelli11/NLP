Course Outline:
Week 1: Introduction to NLP
Course overview and objectives
History and applications of NLP
Basic text processing and tokenization
Week 2: Linguistic Essentials
Morphology: words and their structure
Syntax: sentence structure
Semantics: meaning representation
Week 3: Text Preprocessing
Tokenization, stemming, lemmatization
Stop word removal
Text normalization
Week 4: Language Models
N-grams and smoothing techniques
Neural language models
Word embeddings (Word2Vec, GloVe)
Week 5: Text Classification
Bag-of-Words and TF-IDF
Naive Bayes classifiers
Support Vector Machines and Logistic Regression
Week 6: Sequence Labeling
Part-of-Speech tagging
Named Entity Recognition (NER)
Hidden Markov Models and Conditional Random Fields
Week 7: Syntactic Parsing
Dependency parsing
Constituency parsing
Treebanks and parsing algorithms
Week 8: Semantic Analysis
Semantic role labeling
Word sense disambiguation
Distributional semantics
Week 9: Advanced Neural Models
Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) and Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM)
Transformers and attention mechanisms
BERT and other pre-trained language models
Week 10: Machine Translation
Statistical Machine Translation
Neural Machine Translation
Evaluation of translation systems
Week 11: Sentiment Analysis and Opinion Mining
Sentiment classification
Aspect-based sentiment analysis
Opinion summarization
Week 12: Information Retrieval and Extraction
Search engines and relevance ranking
Information extraction techniques
Question answering systems
Week 13: Dialogue Systems and Chatbots
Task-oriented dialogue systems
Open-domain chatbots
Evaluation of dialogue systems
Week 14: Ethics and Fairness in NLP
Bias in NLP models
Ethical considerations in NLP applications
Fairness, accountability, and transparency
Week 15: Current Trends and Research in NLP
Review of recent NLP research papers
Discussion on future directions
Final project presentations
Assessments:
Homework Assignments (40%): Regular assignments covering implementation and theory.
Midterm Exam (20%): Written exam assessing understanding of core concepts.
Final Project (30%): A comprehensive project on an NLP task, including a written report and presentation.
Class Participation (10%): Active participation in discussions and activities.
Recommended Textbooks and Resources:
"Speech and Language Processing" by Daniel Jurafsky and James H. Martin
"Natural Language Processing with Python" by Steven Bird, Ewan Klein, and Edward Loper
Recent research papers and articles (provided throughout the course)
Tools and Software:
Python (with libraries such as NLTK, SpaCy, Scikit-learn, TensorFlow, PyTorch)
Jupyter Notebooks for assignments and projects
Academic Integrity:
Students are expected to adhere to the highest standards of academic integrity. Plagiarism and cheating will not be tolerated and will result in disciplinary actions.
